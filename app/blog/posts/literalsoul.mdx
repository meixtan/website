A friend's three-year-old daughter recently asked her what a soul is[^1]. She explained that part of it could be the letters and diaries we leave behind. The great promise of our literate society, that we may be immortalized by our words.

I rely on ChatGPT to unscramble and academify my drafty language, and I berate[^2] it sentence by sentence until it sounds just stuffy enough.
The result is an academic stamp of approval for "well-written" manuscripts that I do not consider my soul.


> *The world is full of people with agendas, trying to persuade us to act on their behalf (spend on their behalf, fight and die on their behalf, oppress others on their behalf). But inside us is what Hemingway called a "built-in, shockproof, shit detector". How do we know something is shit? We watch the way the deep, honest part of our mind reacts to it. And that part of the mind is the one that reading and writing refine into sharpness.*

[^1]: This was prompted by Frozen II, a surprisingly deep movie.
[^2]: Do you ever read the grating ChatGPT voice and need* to respond, "ew gross"?
